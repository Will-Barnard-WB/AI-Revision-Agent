# Numerical Analysis Study Guide

## 1. Numerical Methods for Solving Equations
- **Root-Finding Algorithms**: Methods such as the Bisection Method, Newton-Raphson Method, and Secant Method for finding roots of nonlinear equations.
- **Fixed-Point Iteration**: Techniques for solving equations by reformulating them into fixed-point problems.
- **Systems of Linear Equations**: Direct methods (Gaussian elimination, LU decomposition) and iterative methods (Jacobi, Gauss-Seidel, and Successive Over-Relaxation).

## 2. Interpolation
- **Polynomial Interpolation**: Techniques such as Lagrange and Newton interpolation polynomials.
- **Spline Interpolation**: Use of piecewise polynomials (cubic splines) for smooth interpolation.
- **Error Analysis in Interpolation**: Understanding the error bounds and conditions for interpolation methods.

## 3. Numerical Integration
- **Quadrature Rules**: Techniques for approximating definite integrals, including Trapezoidal Rule, Simpson's Rule, and Gaussian Quadrature.
- **Adaptive Quadrature**: Methods that adjust the number of points based on the function's behavior to improve accuracy.
- **Monte Carlo Integration**: Using random sampling to estimate integrals, particularly in high dimensions.

## 4. Numerical Differentiation
- **Finite Difference Methods**: Approximating derivatives using difference quotients (forward, backward, and central differences).
- **Error Analysis**: Understanding truncation errors and stability in numerical differentiation.
- **Higher-Order Derivatives**: Techniques for approximating higher-order derivatives and their applications.

## 5. Error Analysis
- **Types of Errors**: Understanding round-off errors, truncation errors, and their propagation in numerical algorithms.
- **Stability and Convergence**: Analyzing the stability of numerical methods and their convergence properties.
- **Conditioning**: Assessing how the output of a function or algorithm changes in response to small changes in input.

## 6. Additional Topics
- **Numerical Linear Algebra**: Matrix factorizations, eigenvalue problems, and iterative methods for large systems.
- **Optimization**: Numerical methods for finding minima and maxima of functions, including gradient descent and Newton's method.
- **Partial Differential Equations**: Numerical techniques for solving PDEs, such as finite difference and finite element methods.
